# Business Model Framework

## Capture elements

Each element shapes how the website is structured and what content it needs.

### Offer type

What the business sells: product, service, platform, subscription, consulting,
or a combination.

**How to analyze:** Identify whether the offer is standardized or custom,
one-time or recurring, self-contained or part of a larger engagement.

**Impact on site:** Standardized offers need feature/comparison pages. Custom
offers need process and credibility pages. Platforms need demo or trial flows.

### Revenue model

How money flows: project-based, retainer, subscription, usage-based,
transaction fee, licensing.

**How to analyze:** Determine whether revenue is one-time or recurring, and
whether the buyer commits upfront or over time.

**Impact on site:** Recurring models benefit from pricing pages and ROI
calculators. Project-based models need scope and process clarity.

### Sales motion

How deals happen. Determines the entire conversion architecture of the site.

**How to analyze:** Map the typical path from first contact to signed deal.

**Impact on site:** See the sales motion patterns section below.

### Conversion event

The specific action the site drives: form submission, call booking, purchase,
sign-up, demo request, application.

**How to analyze:** Identify the single most valuable action a visitor can take.
Everything else on the site supports this action.

**Impact on site:** The conversion event determines the primary CTA on every page.

### Sales cycle length

Time from first touch to closed deal: instant, days, weeks, months, quarters.

**How to analyze:** Longer cycles need more content to nurture. Shorter cycles
need faster paths to conversion.

**Impact on site:** Short cycles favor direct CTAs. Long cycles need educational
content, multiple touch points, and lead capture.

### Packaging

How the offer is structured for the buyer: single tier, multiple tiers,
modular, bundled, a la carte, custom-scoped.

**How to analyze:** Determine whether the buyer chooses from a menu or gets a
tailored proposal.

**Impact on site:** Tiered packaging benefits from a pricing page. Custom
packaging needs a consultation flow.

### Pricing logic

How the price is determined.

**How to analyze:** Determine the pricing model and whether price is shown on
the site.

**Impact on site:** See pricing logic patterns below.

### Dependencies

What must exist before the offer can be delivered: integrations, data access,
internal team, infrastructure.

**How to analyze:** Dependencies become content for a requirements or
getting-started page.

### Expansion paths

How existing customers grow: upsell, cross-sell, seat expansion, usage growth.

**How to analyze:** Expansion paths inform secondary CTAs and account-level
content.

## Sales motion patterns

### Self-serve

Buyer discovers, evaluates, and purchases without talking to anyone.

- **Site structure:** Product pages, pricing page, comparison tables, sign-up
  flow, documentation
- **Primary CTA:** Sign up, Start free trial, Buy now
- **Content needs:** Feature details, FAQ, integration guides, trust signals
- **Key pages:** Pricing, Features, Docs, Sign-up

### Sales-led

Buyer engages with a sales team before purchasing. Common for high-value B2B.

- **Site structure:** Problem-oriented pages, solutions pages, case studies,
  contact/booking page
- **Primary CTA:** Book a call, Request a demo, Get a consultation
- **Content needs:** Thought leadership, process documentation, proof of
  expertise, qualification content
- **Key pages:** Solutions, About/Team, Case Studies, Contact

### Hybrid

Buyer can self-serve for smaller purchases but engages sales for larger deals.

- **Site structure:** Combines self-serve and sales-led elements. Pricing page
  with "Contact us for enterprise" tier.
- **Primary CTA:** Start free (small), Talk to sales (large)
- **Content needs:** Both product documentation and consultative content
- **Key pages:** Pricing, Features, Solutions, Contact

### Marketplace / platform

Buyer and seller both use the platform. Two-sided content needs.

- **Site structure:** Separate value propositions for each side. Often separate
  landing pages per audience.
- **Primary CTA:** Join as [role], List your [thing], Browse [inventory]
- **Content needs:** Trust signals for both sides, how-it-works for each role
- **Key pages:** How It Works, For Buyers, For Sellers, Browse

## Pricing logic patterns

| Pattern        | Show price? | Site element                                    |
| -------------- | ----------- | ----------------------------------------------- |
| Per-seat       | Yes         | Pricing page with seat calculator               |
| Tiered         | Yes         | Pricing page with plan comparison table          |
| Usage-based    | Partially   | Pricing page with usage examples or calculator   |
| Flat-rate      | Yes         | Pricing page with single clear price             |
| Custom-quoted  | No          | "Get a quote" CTA, scope/process page            |
| Free + paid    | Yes         | Pricing page with free tier highlighted          |

## Decision tree: choosing the conversion path

1. **Is the offer priced below the buyer's approval threshold?**
   - Yes: self-serve checkout is viable.
   - No: go to step 2.

2. **Does the buyer need to evaluate options or get internal buy-in?**
   - Yes: sales-led with content that supports internal advocacy (ROI docs,
     comparison pages, case studies).
   - No: go to step 3.

3. **Is the offer standardized enough to price publicly?**
   - Yes: hybrid model -- show pricing, offer "Talk to sales" for custom needs.
   - No: sales-led with consultation CTA.

4. **Is there a free or low-cost entry point?**
   - Yes: product-led growth -- free trial or freemium as primary CTA.
   - No: direct sales engagement.

## How business model shapes page structure and CTA logic

| Business model signal    | Page structure impact                         | CTA logic                              |
| ------------------------ | --------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------- |
| High-value custom offer  | Process page, case studies, about/team page   | Book a consultation                    |
| Standardized product     | Feature page, pricing page, comparison tables | Start free trial / Buy now             |
| Long sales cycle         | Blog, resources, lead magnets, email capture  | Download guide / Subscribe             |
| Short sales cycle        | Minimal pages, fast path to conversion        | Buy now / Sign up                      |
| Multiple buyer personas  | Persona-specific landing pages or sections    | Role-specific CTAs                     |
| Recurring revenue        | Pricing page with billing toggle, ROI content | Start subscription / See plans         |
| Marketplace              | Two-sided landing pages, trust for both sides | Join as [role]                         |

## Questions for analysis

- Is the site generating leads, demos, calls, purchases, applications, or inquiries?
- What is the main conversion path?
- Is the audience technical, executive, operational, or consumer?
- What information must exist on the site to move the buyer forward?
- How much does the buyer need to trust the business before converting?
- Is the purchase decision made by one person or a committee?
- Does the buyer compare alternatives before deciding?
- What is the average deal value and does it justify a sales team?
